Sonnet 73 William Shakespeare Aging, love, time The speaker compares himself to the late autumn season, using metaphors of a dying fire and a setting sun to illustrate his feelings of aging and mortality. He expresses his love for his beloved, who cherishes him despite his approaching end. Renaissance (Elizabethan) Metaphor, simile, imagery, sonnet form Part of the Fair Youth sonnets
Sonnet 141 William Shakespeare Love, desire, conflict The speaker acknowledges his overwhelming desire for his beloved, even though he knows his love is not reciprocated in the same way. He feels trapped by his attraction to her, despite knowing it may lead to pain. Renaissance (Elizabethan) Irony, paradox, personification, sonnet form Part of the Dark Lady sonnets
Batter My Heart (Holy Sonnet) John Donne Religion, spiritual conflict, divine love The speaker pleads with God to forcefully break down his resistance and make him worthy of divine love. He uses violent imagery of siege warfare to illustrate his desire for a complete spiritual transformation. Renaissance (Jacobean) Metaphysical conceit, imagery, paradox, sonnet form Part of the Holy Sonnets
Song: Go And Catch A Falling Star John Donne Love, unattainable ideals, female virtue The speaker sets a series of impossible tasks, including finding a falling star and a woman who is both beautiful and faithful. He concludes that such perfection is unattainable and human nature is inherently flawed. Renaissance (Jacobean) Hyperbole, metaphor, irony, paradox Characteristic of metaphysical poetry
To the Memory of Mr. Oldham John Dryden Death, loss, mourning, poetic legacy The speaker mourns the untimely death of fellow poet John Oldham, praising his talent and lamenting the loss of his potential. He uses classical allusions and imagery to elevate Oldham's status and ensure his poetic immortality. Restoration Elegy, metaphor, allusion, personification Dedicated to a fellow poet
An extract from Canto 3 - Rape Of The Lock Alexander Pope Vanity, society, superficiality The extract depicts the card game between Belinda and the Baron, highlighting the trivial pursuits and social rituals of the upper class. The game becomes a mock-epic battle, showcasing the absurdity of their vanity and obsession with appearances. 18th Century Mock-epic, satire, imagery, allegory Part of a longer satirical poem
Chimney Sweeper William Blake Innocence, social injustice, child labor The poem tells the story of a young chimney sweep, highlighting the harsh realities of child labor during the Industrial Revolution. It explores themes of innocence lost, exploitation, and the false hope offered by religion. Romantic Irony, symbolism, social commentary Part of Songs of Innocence
Ode To A Nightingale John Keats Nature, beauty, mortality, escapism The speaker, overwhelmed by the nightingale's song, longs to escape the human world of suffering and mortality. He explores themes of beauty, fleeting pleasure, and the power of imagination to transcend reality. Romantic Ode form, imagery, symbolism, alliteration, assonance One of Keats's most famous odes
A Slumber Did My Spirit Seal William Wordsworth Nature, death, immortality, love The speaker reflects on the death of a loved one, "Lucy," and finds solace in the idea that she is now unified with nature. The poem explores themes of mortality, the permanence of nature, and the transformative power of death. Romantic Imagery, juxtaposition, simplicity, lyrical ballad form Part of the Lucy poems
To A Snow Drop William Wordsworth Nature, resilience, beauty, transience The speaker addresses a snowdrop flower, admiring its delicate beauty and resilience in the face of winter's harshness. The poem reflects on the cyclical nature of life and the enduring power of nature. Romantic Personification, imagery, sonnet form Celebrates the beauty of a small flower
Remember Christina Rossetti Love, death, memory, grief The speaker, anticipating their own death, asks their beloved to remember them. The poem explores the tension between the desire to be remembered and the eventual peace that comes with being forgotten. Victorian Sonnet form, repetition, imagery, melancholy tone Explores the complexities of grief and memory
Among School Children William Butler Yeats Memory, aging, beauty, the passage of time The speaker, visiting a schoolroom, is prompted to reflect on his own childhood and the fleeting nature of beauty and youth. The poem explores the cyclical nature of life and the interconnectedness of past, present, and future. Modernist Imagery, symbolism, philosophical musings Reflects on the nature of time and memory
Suicide In The Trenches Siegfried Sassoon War, trauma, disillusionment, social commentary The poem tells the story of a young soldier who commits suicide, highlighting the brutal realities of trench warfare and its psychological impact. It criticizes the glorification of war and exposes its devastating consequences. Modernist (War Poetry) Irony, stark imagery, colloquial language, anti-war sentiment Direct and powerful condemnation of war
Morning At The Window T.S. Eliot Urban life, alienation, modernity, isolation The speaker observes the dreary cityscape from his window, capturing the sense of alienation and isolation prevalent in modern urban life. The poem utilizes fragmented imagery and a detached tone to convey the bleakness of the urban experience. Modernist Imagery, symbolism, fragmentation, free verse Captures the disillusionment of modern life
Design Robert Frost Nature, fate, chance, the meaning of existence The speaker contemplates the chance encounter of a spider, a moth, and a flower, questioning whether there is any purposeful design behind this seemingly insignificant event. The poem explores themes of fate, chance, and the ambiguous nature of the universe. Modernist Imagery, symbolism, sonnet form, philosophical questioning Reflects on the meaning of random events
Money Philip Larkin Materialism, desire, class, social commentary The speaker reflects on the allure and limitations of money, recognizing its power to bring comfort and security but also its inability to truly fulfill human desires for happiness and meaning. Post-War/Modernist Irony, colloquial language, social commentary, reflective tone Critiques the materialistic values of society
The Cathedral Builders John Ormond Labor, legacy, time, human ambition The poem explores the lives of the men who built cathedrals, highlighting their dedication, skill, and the enduring legacy they leave behind. It reflects on the passage of time and the significance of human endeavor in creating lasting works of art. Post-War/Modernist Imagery, symbolism, historical reflection, free verse Celebrates the artistry and dedication of craftsmen
An Introduction Kamala Das Identity, language, gender, societal expectations The speaker reflects on her identity as an Indian woman writing in English, challenging societal expectations and asserting her right to self-expression. The poem explores themes of language, cultural hybridity, and the search for authenticity. Post-Colonial/Modernist Free verse, personal voice, feminist themes, cultural commentary Explores the complexities of identity and language
An Unknown Girl Moniza Alvi Identity, culture, belonging, displacement The speaker, a British woman of Pakistani descent, encounters a henna artist in India and reflects on her own fragmented sense of identity. The poem explores themes of cultural hybridity, the search for roots, and the experience of feeling caught between two cultures. Post-Colonial/Contemporary Imagery, symbolism, cultural exploration, personal reflection Examines the complexities of cultural belonging
Phenominal Woman Maya Angelou Self-love, confidence, femininity, beauty standards The speaker celebrates her own unique beauty and femininity, challenging conventional beauty standards and embracing her own power and confidence. The poem is an anthem of self-acceptance and empowerment. Contemporary Free verse, repetition, empowering tone, feminist themes Celebrates female strength and individuality
The Fisherman Mourned By His Wife Patrick Fernando Loss, grief, love, the fragility of life The poem portrays a wife mourning the death of her fisherman husband, reflecting on their life together and the harsh realities of their existence. It explores themes of love, loss, and the unpredictable nature of life. Post-Colonial/Contemporary Elegy, imagery, symbolism, personal reflection, Sri Lankan context Explores the impact of sudden death on loved ones
Animal Crackers Richard De Zoysa Violence, social commentary, political satire, Sri Lankan context The poem uses the metaphor of animal crackers to satirize the violence and political turmoil during the Sri Lankan civil war. It critiques the complicity of those in power and the suffering of ordinary people. Post-Colonial/Contemporary Metaphor, satire, dark humor, political commentary Reflects on the brutal realities of the Sri Lankan civil war
Explosion Vivimarie Vanderpoorten Trauma, violence, memory, Sri Lankan context The poem explores the psychological impact of a bomb explosion, capturing the fragmentation of memory, the sense of shock and disorientation, and the lingering trauma. It reflects on the violence and instability of the Sri Lankan civil war. Post-Colonial/Contemporary Free verse, imagery, fragmentation, sensory details, personal reflection Explores the lasting effects of trauma and violence
Spring And Fall (To A Young Child) Gerard Manley Hopkins Nature, mortality, innocence, the passage of time The speaker addresses a child who is weeping over the falling leaves in autumn, explaining that her grief stems from an unconscious awareness of her own mortality. The poem explores the beauty and fragility of life and the inevitability of death. Victorian Nature imagery, symbolism, sprung rhythm, philosophical reflection Connects the natural world with human experience
